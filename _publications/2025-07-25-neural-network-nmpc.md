This work addresses full-flight-envelope trajectory tracking for a tail-sitter VTOL UAV using nonlinear model predictive control with a neural-network aerodynamic predictive model. The model retains a compact nonlinear aerodynamic representation to balance prediction accuracy and real-time optimization complexity.

Simulation results show that the proposed controller substantially improves trajectory tracking accuracy while maintaining real-time performance, providing a promising path toward deployment on real aircraft.

**Yuying Zhang is the second author.**
